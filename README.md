# SplitWatch-Support

**SplitWatch** is a professional timing app built for coaches, officials, and athletes who need precise single-event tracking or simultaneous multi-athlete timing.

---

## How to use SplitWatch

### Single Stopwatch Mode
Switch to the **Stopwatch** tab for quick, single-athlete or solo training sessions.
1. Tap **Start** to begin timing.
2. Tap **Lap** while running to log splits. The app automatically highlights your fastest and slowest laps in green and red.
3. Tap **Stop** to pause timing.
4. While paused, tap **Save** to title the session and log it securely to your history, or tap **Reset** to clear.

### Multi-Stopwatch Mode
Switch to the **Multiwatch** tab to time whole heats or groups simultaneously.
1. Tap **+** to add as many stopwatch cards as you need. New cards are added directly to the top for easy access.
2. Press **Start All** at the bottom bar when the race begins to start every timer synchronously.
3. Tap **Lap** on individual cards as athletes pass split markers, or use **Lap All** to log a split across all running clocks.
4. Press **Stop** on individual cards as athletes finish, or use **Stop All**.
5. Tap **Save** in the bottom control bar, title your session (e.g., "1600m Heat 1"), and it will instantly sync to your history.

### History Mode
Switch to the **History** tab to review, manage, and audit your saved timing sessions.
1. Tap any athlete's card block to expand it and review their full list of individual lap split times.
2. Tap the **Edit (Pencil)** icon in the top right of any session to update athlete names or correct timing mistakes. 
3. Delete or adjust accidental splits within the editor—choose to discard the time entirely or merge it into adjacent laps to keep your total time mathematically accurate.
4. Tap the **✕** icon on the main screen to permanently delete an entire session block from your device storage.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can I start and stop stopwatches individually in multi-mode?**
Yes. Every stopwatch card operates independently with its own Start, Stop, Lap, and Reset actions. The global bottom bar provides synchronized controls for convenience, but you can manage any card on its own at any time.

**Can I correct mistakes or edit lap times after a session is saved?**
Yes. If you made a timing error or want to change a runner's name after saving, open the **History** tab and tap the **Edit (Pencil)** icon on any session block. From there, you can select any athlete to rewrite names, modify split times, or remove laps entirely (with options to add the time back to adjacent laps so your cumulative totals stay accurate).

**Does SplitWatch scale nicely on tablets and iPads?**
Yes. The entire user interface features responsive scaling dynamic up to 2.0x baseline proportions. Text elements, layout paddings, and control circles expand uniformly to maximize real estate and readability on massive screens.

**Is it easy to tell that a button was pressed?**
Yes. Stopwatch cards flash white/black when lap is pressed, red when stop is pressed, green when start is pressed, and white/black when reset is pressed. If you have haptics turned on, the phone will slightly vibrate. Lap text sizes are also increased to make seeing real-time splits easier.

**Is my data saved if I close the app?**
Yes. All session history is safely stored on your device's local storage and persists even if the app is fully closed or restarted.

**Can I rename a stopwatch during a live session?**
Yes. Tap the title name on any active stopwatch card to change it instantly.

**Can I reorder the stopwatches?**
Yes. Click and hold the icon with three lines on the top left of the stopwatch card, then drag the stopwatch to the desired position on the page.

**Does SplitWatch require an internet connection?**
No. SplitWatch operates completely offline. No accounts, sign-ins, or cloud dependencies are required.

**How do I delete a session from history?**
Open the History tab and press the **✕** icon in the top-right corner of the session block you wish to remove.
